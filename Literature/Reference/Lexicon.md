A personal repository of linguistically significant words and their usage.

- **Contemporaneous**: Existing or occurring in the same period of time.
- **Idiosyncratic**: Peculiar or individual.
- **Vicissitudes**: A change of fortunes or circumstances, typically one that is unwelcome or unpleasant.
- **Impetus**: The force or energy with which a body moves.
- **Inundation**: An overwhelming abundance of people or things.
- **Cognizant**: Having knowledge or being aware of.
- **Adduce**: Cite as evidence.
- **Preceptor**: A teacher or instructor.
- **Perversity**: A deliberate desire to behave in an unreasonable or unacceptable way.
- **Frugal**: Sparing or economical with regard to money or food.
- **Cynosure**: A person or thing that is the center of attention or admiration.
- **Anathema**: Something or someone that one vehemently dislikes.
- **Preponderance**: The quality or fact of being greater in number, quantity, or importance.
- **Diminutive**: Extremely or unusually small.
- **Languid**: (of a person, manner, or gesture) displaying or having a disinclination for physical exertion or effort; slow and relaxed.
- **Inauspicious**: Not conducive to success; unpromising.
- **Satiable**: Able to be satisfied.
- **Austere**: Severe or strict in manner, attitude, or appearance.
- **Prodigious**: Remarkably or impressively great in extent, size, or degree.
- **Didactic**: Intended to teach, particularly in having moral instruction as an ulterior motive.
- **Deference**: Humble submission and respect.
- **Pre-eminence**: The fact of surpassing all others; superiority.
- **Clandestine**: Kept secret or done secretively, especially because illicit.
- **Thither**: To or toward that place.
- **Wanton**: (of a cruel or violent action) deliberate and unprovoked.
- **Interposition**: The action of interposing someone or something.
- **Limpid**: (of a liquid) free of anything that darkens; completely clear.
- **Effable**: Able to be described in words.
- **Ineffable**: Too great or extreme to be expressed or described in words.
- **Serried**: (of rows of people or things) standing close together.
- **Despondent**: In low spirits from loss of hope or courage.
- **Vexed**: (of a problem or issue) difficult and much debated; problematic.
- **Impertinence**: Lack of respect; rudeness.
- **Assuage**: Make (an unpleasant feeling) less intense.
- **Ardour**: Enthusiasm or passion.
- **Lamentable**: (of circumstances or conditions) deplorably bad or unsatisfactory.
- **Imprecate**: Utter (a curse) or invoke (evil) against someone or something.
- **Envisage**: Contemplate or conceive of as a possibility or a desirable future event.
- **Abjure**: Solemnly renounce (a belief, cause, or claim).
- **Diffident**: Modest or shy because of a lack of self-confidence.
- **Importune**: Ask (someone) pressingly and persistently for or to do something.
- **Connivance**: Willingness to allow or be secretly involved in an immoral or illegal act.
- **Carte blanche**: Complete freedom to act as one wishes or thinks best.
- **Iniquity**: Immoral or grossly unfair behavior.
- **Asinine**: Extremely stupid or foolish.
- **Halcyon**: Denoting a period of time in the past that was idyllically happy and peaceful.
