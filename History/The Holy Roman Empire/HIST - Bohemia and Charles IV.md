### HIST - Bohemia and the Golden Age of Charles IV

## The Jewel of Central Europe

Playing Kingdom Come Deliverance nowadays. The game world is so immersive that I feel compelled to read up on the history of the time it is set during.

Let's begin with the state of Europe at the time. To put it bluntly, Europe was more or less a **fractured continent**, a continent in perpetual motion. There were no neat borders, no unified nations as we understand them today. Instead, kingdoms overlapped with duchies, church territories carved out their own domains, and the Holy Roman Empire stretched across Central Europe like a threadbare tapestry.

The Empire itself was less a coherent state than a political fiction maintained by mutual agreement. Real power belonged not to the emperor but to the regional princes and the seven prince-electors who chose him. In the west, France and England circled each other with growing hostility, their conflict soon to ignite into the **Hundred Years' War**. Meanwhile, an older struggle continued: the eternal question of whether popes or kings truly ruled Christian Europe.

But perhaps the deepest crisis afflicting the continent was [[HIST - The Great Western Schism|spiritual in nature]]. In 1309, Pope Clement V moved the entire papal court to Avignon. For seventy years, the papacy would remain there, growing more entangled with French interests. To Germans, English, and Italians, the papacy had become a French puppet.

Then came the famines of 1315 to 1317, and in 1347, the Black Death arrived. It swept through Europe like a scythe through wheat, killing between a third and half of the population. old certainties crumbled, and peasants began to question the order that had kept them in their place.

It was in this fractured, plague-haunted, spiritually adrift Europe that one principality began to shine with unusual brilliance: **Bohemia, the jewel of Central Europe.**

## The Golden Age: Charles IV and the Making of Prague

Charles of Luxembourg was born in 1316. He became King of Bohemia in 1346 and Holy Roman Emperor in 1355. He understood that power required more than armies; it required legitimacy, culture, and institutions.

In 1348, he founded Charles University in Prague, the first university in Central Europe. He rebuilt Prague Castle and began construction of the Charles Bridge. In 1356, he issued the Golden Bull, establishing how emperors would be chosen.

Bohemia under Charles IV became rich, powerful, and culturally vibrant. When Charles died in 1378, he left behind what many considered a golden age.

## Wenceslas IV and the Unraveling

Wenceslas IV inherited his father's throne but not his father's gifts. He was indecisive and weak. In 1400, the German princes deposed him as King of the Romans. Under his rule, Bohemia's golden age tarnished, and ethnic tensions between Czechs and Germans grew sharper.

## The Ambitious Brother: Sigismund's Hungarian Power Base

Sigismund was cunning and ruthlessly pragmatic. While his brother struggled, Sigismund carved out his domain in Hungary, leaning heavily on his **Cuman** cavalry to consolidate power.

## The Church Torn Apart: The Great Western Schism

The year Charles IV died—1378—was the same year the Catholic Church split. By 1409, there were three popes. In Bohemia, a preacher named Jan Hus began to ask dangerous questions about Church corruption.
